How I Built It

Why I Made the Compatibility Tool

A friendship that didn't make sense on paper — until I ran the numbers

By Quantum Merlin
February 2026

I never planned to build a compatibility tool. Honestly, I thought compatibility calculators were the cheesiest part of astrology. "Enter your sign and your crush's sign and find out if you're meant to be!" That stuff drove me nuts. It was shallow, misleading, and it reduced something complex into a percentage that meant nothing.

Then I tried to figure out my best friend.

The Friendship That Broke the Model

His name's not important. What matters is that we've been inseparable for over a decade, and on paper, we shouldn't work at all.

I'm a Life Path 7. He's a 3. I'm a water sign. He's fire. My Chinese zodiac animal and his are supposedly incompatible. If you ran us through any standard compatibility calculator — the kind I just said I hated — we'd score somewhere around "politely avoid each other."

But in real life? We finish each other's sentences. We've navigated life crises together without a single serious fight. Our friendship is one of the most stable things in either of our lives.

That gap — between what the systems predicted and what was obviously true — bugged me. It bugged me for months. Because either the systems were rubbish (possible) or I was reading them wrong (more likely).

Looking in the Wrong Place

Here's what I was doing wrong, and it's what every compatibility calculator does wrong.

I was comparing surface traits. Sun sign vs sun sign. Life Path vs Life Path. That's like comparing two people's job titles and deciding whether they'd be good flatmates. It tells you almost nothing about the actual dynamic.

So I went deeper. I ran both our full charts — Western astrology with all the planets, full numerology profiles, Chinese zodiac with elements, moon phases. And instead of looking at the obvious stuff, I started looking at the layers.

That's when I found it.

Our Soul Urge numbers were identical. Soul Urge is the number calculated from the vowels in your full birth name — and it represents the thing you want most deeply, the need under everything else. The part of you that almost nobody sees because it's too vulnerable to show.

We had the same one. Identical. And I'd never thought to look there because every compatibility tool I'd ever seen compared the showy stuff — the sun signs, the life paths, the public-facing numbers. Nobody was comparing the hidden layers.

Building the Seven Dimensions

That discovery changed everything. I realised compatibility isn't one score. It's not even two or three scores. It's a multi-layered thing, and the layers matter differently depending on the type of relationship.

So I built a system with seven independent dimensions:

  1. Life Path Harmony — are your core life directions pulling the same way?
  2. Emotional Resonance — do you process feelings in compatible ways?
  3. Intellectual Compatibility — do your minds stimulate each other?
  4. Communication Style — this is where most conflicts actually live
  5. Chinese Zodiac Dynamic — elemental interaction, not just animal matching
  6. Moon Phase Alignment — the emotional undercurrents
  7. Karmic Connection — the deeper patterns that suggest why you're in each other's lives at all

Each dimension is scored independently. And that independence is the whole point. Because a couple who scores 95% on emotional resonance but 35% on communication isn't "65% compatible." They're deeply connected people who keep having the same argument. That's useful information. An average hides it.

The Pattern I Didn't Expect

After building the tool, I ran it on every relationship I had data for. Mine and my partner's. My parents'. Friends who were close, friends who'd drifted, colleagues I clicked with, colleagues I couldn't stand.

And a pattern emerged that I genuinely wasn't looking for.

The strongest, most enduring relationships weren't the ones with the highest total scores. They were the ones with the most interesting distribution. High in some areas, low in others, with at least one dimension above 85%.

The relationships that had all 7s and 8s across the board? Nice but boring. The ones with 9s and 3s? Electric. Complex. Difficult sometimes. But the kind of connection where both people grow.

I'm not saying that's a universal rule. But I've run thousands of these now, and I keep seeing it. The best relationships aren't the smoothest ones. They're the ones with enough contrast to keep you awake.

What Surprised Me Most

Honestly? How emotional people get when they run their parents' compatibility.

I didn't build a "run your mum and dad" feature. There's no button for it. But people figured it out immediately — you can enter any two birth dates. And within the first week, I started getting messages from people who'd run their parents and suddenly understood something about their childhood they'd never been able to articulate.

"My parents score 92% on emotional resonance but 28% on communication. That's literally my entire childhood in two numbers."

That message hit me hard. Because that's exactly what happened when I ran my own parents. And it explained so much — the love that was clearly there, and the arguments that never seemed to resolve. The love was real. The language was just different.

Try It Yourself

The compatibility tool is free. Always will be. Enter two birth dates and let the seven dimensions do their thing.

My only advice: don't run it on your crush at 2am when you're feeling vulnerable. Run it on someone you already know well — someone where the relationship has history and texture. Because the power of this tool isn't prediction. It's recognition. When you see a pattern score that explains something you've been living with for years, that's the moment.

That's the "how did it know that?" moment.

And once you've felt it, you'll probably run everyone you know through it. I did. I'm not sorry about it.

— QM, Melbourne

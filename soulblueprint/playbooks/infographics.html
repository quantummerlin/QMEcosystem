üîß Troubleshooting

My infographic looks too cluttered and hard to read

Less is more. Remove any text that isn't essential. Each data point should have a maximum of 1 sentence. If a section has more than 4 bullet points, cut it to the top 3. White space is your friend ‚Äî increase padding between elements. Try switching from a detailed layout to a simpler chart type.

The AI tool isn't generating what I described

Break your prompt into two parts. First, describe only the layout and chart type ("Create a horizontal bar chart with 4 bars"). Once that's right, follow up with content details ("Now add these labels and values‚Ä¶"). AI handles sequential instructions better than one massive prompt.

My charts don't look consistent across the set

Pick a colour palette before you start and use the exact same hex codes in every infographic. Choose one font pair (heading + body) and stick with it. Use the same background style (dark cosmic or light clean) across all infographics. Consistency comes from these three elements: colour, typography, background.

I'm not sure how to rate my Life Pillars (0‚Äì100%)

Read each angular house interpretation carefully. If the reading emphasises gifts, strengths, and ease, rate it 70‚Äì90%. If it emphasises challenges, lessons, and growth areas, rate it 30‚Äì50%. A balanced mix of both? Rate it 50‚Äì70%. These are subjective ‚Äî trust your gut feeling about each reading's tone.

I don't have all the readings needed for an infographic

Skip that infographic and move to the next one. Not every blueprint includes every possible reading. Focus on the infographics where you have complete data. You can always come back and generate missing readings later, then create the remaining infographics.

My infographics look great but nobody is sharing them

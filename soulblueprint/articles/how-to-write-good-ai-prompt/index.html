Why Your First Prompts Probably Didn't Work

Here's what most beginners type into ChatGPT:

‚ùå Vague prompt (bad) 
Tell me about my personality

The AI doesn't know who you are, what kind of personality analysis you want, or how you want it presented. So it gives a generic, wishy-washy response.

Now compare it with this:

‚úÖ Structured prompt (great) 
You are a personality analyst. I'm going to paste my Soul Blueprint reading below. Please extract:

1. My top 5 personality strengths (3-5 words each)
2. My top 3 challenges to watch for
3. One sentence that captures my core identity

Format as a clean bulleted list.

Here is my reading:
[PASTE READING HERE]

Night and day difference. The second prompt tells the AI exactly who to be, what to do, what to work with, and how to format the answer.

The 4-Part Prompt Formula

Every great AI prompt has four ingredients:

Part What It Does Example
üé≠ Role Tells AI who to be "You are a personality analyst"
üìã Task Tells AI what to do "Extract the top 5 strengths"
üìé Context Gives AI material to work with "Here is my personality reading: [paste]"
üìê Format Tells AI how to structure the output "Format as a bulleted list"

You don't need all four every time ‚Äî but the more you include, the better your results. Task is the most important. Always be specific about what you want.

üí° Memory trick Think: R-T-C-F ‚Äî Role, Task, Context, Format. Or just remember: "Tell it who to be, what to do, what to work with, and how to show it."

The #1 Mistake That Ruins Good Prompts

Being too vague about format.

If you don't tell AI how to structure its response, it'll write a wall of text. Always specify:

  • "Format as a bulleted list"
  • "Keep each point under 10 words"
  • "Use a table with columns for X and Y"
  • "Write exactly 3 paragraphs"
  • "Number the items"

This one change alone will make your AI results 5x more useful.

‚ö†Ô∏è Also avoid Don't ask AI to do too many things in one prompt. If you want a summary AND social posts AND journal prompts, make those three separate prompts. One task per prompt = better results.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Too much text per slide. Each slide should have one headline and 1‚Äì2 short sentences max. If you can't read it in 3 seconds, it's too much. People swipe fast.
  2. Inconsistent design across slides. If slide 3 has a different font, color, or layout than slide 7, the carousel feels unprofessional. Use Canva's "Duplicate page" feature and just swap the text.
  3. Weak hook slide. If slide 1 doesn't stop the scroll, nobody sees slides 2‚Äì10. Spend 50% of your effort on making slide 1 irresistible ‚Äî bold text, provocative question, or a surprising stat.
  4. No CTA on the last slide. Always tell people what to do next: "Get your free reading," "Save for later," or "Tag your Scorpio friend." Without a CTA, engagement dies at slide 10.
  5. Using the wrong aspect ratio. Carousels should be 1080√ó1080px (square) or 1080√ó1350px (portrait). Never use landscape ‚Äî it wastes feed real estate and looks amateur.

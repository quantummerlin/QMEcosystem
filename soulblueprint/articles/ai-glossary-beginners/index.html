Everyday AI Terms

1. AI (Artificial Intelligence)

Software that can perform tasks that normally require human thinking â€” like writing, analysing patterns, or making decisions. When people say "AI" in 2026, they usually mean chatbots like ChatGPT or Gemini.

2. Chatbot

A program you can have a text conversation with. ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude are all chatbots. You type, they respond.

3. Prompt

The message you type to an AI. Your prompt is your instruction. Better prompts = better results. Learn the formula â†’

4. Response / Output

What the AI writes back after you give it a prompt. Also called "output" or "generation".

5. Hallucination

When AI confidently makes something up that isn't true. It sounds convincing but is factually wrong. This is why you should always verify important facts. Learn why this happens â†’

6. Model

The "brain" behind the AI. GPT-4o is a model. Gemini 2.0 is a model. Different models have different strengths. When someone says "which model are you using?", they're asking which AI brain powers the tool.

7. Token

The unit AI uses to measure text. One token â‰ˆ Â¾ of a word. When a tool says "4,000 token limit", it means roughly 3,000 words. You usually don't need to worry about this on free tiers.

8. Context Window

How much text the AI can "remember" in a single conversation. A larger context window means it can handle longer documents or conversations without forgetting earlier parts.

How AI Works (Behind the Scenes)

9. LLM (Large Language Model)

The technical name for AI chatbots like ChatGPT. "Large" because it was trained on massive amounts of text. "Language" because it works with words. "Model" because it's a mathematical pattern-matcher.

10. Training

How an AI learns. During training, the AI reads billions of text examples and learns patterns â€” like how sentences are structured, what usually follows what, and how different topics connect.

11. Training Data

The text the AI learned from. This includes books, websites, articles, and more. The quality and breadth of training data determines how good the AI is.

12. Fine-Tuning

Extra training on specific content to make an AI better at particular tasks. Think of it as specialisation after general education.

13. Parameters

The internal "settings" inside an AI model's brain. GPT-4 has over a trillion parameters. More parameters generally = more capable (but also more expensive to run).

14. Neural Network

The architecture inside AI that's loosely inspired by how brain neurons connect. Information flows through layers of "neurons" that each process a small part of the task.

15. Deep Learning

A type of AI that uses many layers in its neural network (hence "deep"). This is the technology behind all modern AI chatbots.

Using AI Better

16. Temperature

A setting that controls how creative vs. predictable the AI's responses are. Low temperature = factual and consistent. High temperature = creative and surprising. Most chatbots handle this automatically.

17. System Prompt

Hidden instructions that tell the AI how to behave. When you use ChatGPT, there's a system prompt you don't see that says things like "be helpful and harmless". Some tools let you set your own.

18. Few-Shot Prompting

Giving the AI a few examples of what you want before asking it to do the task. Like showing someone 3 example emails before asking them to write one in the same style.

19. Chain of Thought

Asking the AI to "think step by step" before answering. This often produces better results for complex questions because it forces the AI to reason through each part.

20. Zero-Shot

Asking the AI to do something with no examples â€” just the instruction. Most casual prompts are zero-shot: "Write me a poem about the ocean."

Types of AI

21. Generative AI

AI that creates new content â€” text, images, music, code. ChatGPT generates text. DALL-E generates images. All the tools we discuss are generative AI.

22. Multimodal

AI that can handle multiple types of input/output â€” text AND images AND audio. Gemini is multimodal because you can upload photos and get text responses about them.

23. NLP (Natural Language Processing)

The branch of AI focused on understanding human language. Every chatbot uses NLP to understand your prompts and generate natural-sounding responses.

24. Computer Vision

AI that can "see" and understand images. When you upload a photo to Gemini and it describes what's in it â€” that's computer vision.

25. AGI (Artificial General Intelligence)

The theoretical future AI that could do anything a human can do. We don't have this yet. Current AI is "narrow" â€” great at specific tasks, but can't truly think or understand like humans.

AI Safety & Ethics

26. Bias

When AI reflects unfair patterns from its training data. If the data over-represents certain perspectives, the AI might too. This is why diverse training data matters.

27. Guardrails

Rules built into AI to prevent harmful outputs. When ChatGPT refuses to help with something dangerous, that's a guardrail at work.

28. Alignment

Making sure AI does what humans actually want it to do. "Aligned" AI is helpful, harmless, and honest. This is one of the biggest challenges in AI development.

29. RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback)

A training method where humans rate AI responses as good or bad, and the AI learns from those ratings. This is how ChatGPT learned to be helpful and conversational.

30. Open Source

AI models whose code is publicly available for anyone to use, modify, or study. Meta's LLaMA is open-source. ChatGPT is not (it's "closed-source" or "proprietary").

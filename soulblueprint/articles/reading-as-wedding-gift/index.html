Framing and Presentation

Presentation is everything with a wedding gift. Elevate your poster and card with thoughtful framing:

  • Frame choice: White or gold frames suit most wedding aesthetics. Black frames work for modern couples. Avoid overly ornate frames ‚Äî let the poster be the star.
  • Print quality: Use matte paper at a copy shop for a premium feel. Glossy can work but matte looks more elegant and professional.
  • Matting: If your budget allows, add a white mat border around the print before framing. This instantly makes it look gallery-quality. Cost: ~$3‚Äì5 at a craft store.
  • Wrapping: Wrap the framed print in tissue paper and place in a gift bag. Include the card on top. Add a small tag that says "Your Cosmic Connection ‚Äî Mapped" or similar.
‚ö†Ô∏è Know the couple's vibe This gift works beautifully for couples who enjoy personality insights, self-discovery, or cosmic themes. If the couple is strongly skeptical of astrology, reframe it as a "personality compatibility portrait" rather than a zodiac reading. Focus on the personality traits and relationship dynamics rather than astrological terminology.

Timing: When to Give the Gift

When you give the gift matters as much as what you give:

  • Pre-wedding (engagement party or bridal shower): Great timing ‚Äî the couple can enjoy it without the chaos of the wedding day. It stands out more in a smaller setting.
  • At the reception (gift table): Classic approach. Include a note encouraging them to open it at home when they can appreciate it privately. Wedding-day gift opening is rushed.
  • Post-wedding (1‚Äì2 weeks after): Often the best timing. The couple has returned from their honeymoon, they're settling into their new home, and a thoughtful late gift feels like a wonderful surprise when the wedding buzz has faded.
  • Anniversary: Mail it to arrive on the exact date, or give it in person over dinner. Anniversaries are more intimate, so the gift gets the attention it deserves.
üí° Post-wedding is often best Couples receive dozens of gifts on their wedding day and open them in a blur. A beautifully presented gift arriving a week or two later ‚Äî when they're back from the honeymoon and settling in ‚Äî gets much more attention, appreciation, and emotional impact.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Including relationship challenges in the gift. A wedding gift should be 100% celebratory. If the compatibility analysis mentions growth areas or friction, edit them out. This is a celebration, not counselling.
  2. Making the poster too data-heavy. A wedding poster should feel romantic and elegant. Include the highlights ‚Äî names, date, shared strengths, relationship superpower ‚Äî not every planetary placement.
  3. Rushing the design. Start at least 2 weeks before the wedding. You need time to generate readings, create the analysis, design in Canva, print, frame, and wrap. Last-minute execution shows.
  4. Forgetting to explain the gift. Include a brief note explaining what a Soul Blueprint is and how you created this. Without context, even the most beautiful poster might confuse the couple.
  5. Using the wrong birth data. Triple-check both birth dates. A compatibility reading based on incorrect data is awkward to explain at a wedding.

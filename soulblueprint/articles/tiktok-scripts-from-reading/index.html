Master the Hook-First Structure

On TikTok, you have exactly 1.5 seconds before someone scrolls past. Your hook determines everything. Here's how to structure every script:

  1. Hook (0‚Äì3 seconds): The most surprising, provocative, or relatable line. "My AI personality reading said I'm emotionally unavailable because of my Moon sign and I feel attacked" ‚Äî this is the scroll-stopper
  2. Context (3‚Äì8 seconds): Brief setup ‚Äî "So I used this free tool that analyzes your birth chart and personality‚Ä¶"
  3. Payload (8‚Äì25 seconds): The actual insight, delivered with energy and specificity. This is where the reading content shines
  4. Reaction/twist (25‚Äì35 seconds): Your genuine reaction, a surprising detail, or the "wait it gets worse" escalation
  5. CTA (35‚Äì45 seconds): "Link in bio to get yours" or "Comment your sign and I'll tell you what it means"
‚ö†Ô∏è Never start with "Hey guys" or "So today I wanted to‚Ä¶" These openings are instant scroll triggers. Always start with the most interesting part of the content. If your best line is in the middle of the script, move it to the front.

Trending audio gives your video a discoverability boost. Here's how to match audio to script format:

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Scripts that are too long. If your script takes more than 45 seconds to read aloud, cut it in half. TikTok rewards watch-through rate ‚Äî shorter videos that people rewatch beat longer videos that people abandon.
  2. Generic zodiac content. "Leos are confident" is boring. "Leos will rehearse a casual text for 20 minutes then send it and immediately put their phone face-down" is specific, funny, and shareable. Specificity is everything.
  3. Forgetting text overlays. 80% of TikTok users watch without sound. If your video is voice-only with no text on screen, you're invisible to most viewers. Always add key phrases as text overlays.
  4. Not using a hook. Starting with context instead of the hook is the #1 script mistake. Put the most interesting line FIRST, then explain. Always.
  5. Posting at random times. Zodiac content performs best at 7‚Äì9pm weekdays and 10am‚Äì12pm weekends. Use TikTok Analytics (available on Creator accounts, free) to find when YOUR audience is online.

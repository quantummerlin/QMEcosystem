Step-by-Step Brand Building Process

Understand What a Personal Brand Actually Is

A personal brand isn't a logo or a colour palette. It's the answer to three questions:

  • What do you stand for? (Mission ‚Äî your North Node and Sun sign)
  • What makes you different? (Unique angle ‚Äî your Rising sign, Lilith, and elemental balance)
  • Why should people trust you? (Origin story ‚Äî your Chiron wound and how you've grown through it)

Most people try to answer these questions by brainstorming in a vacuum. But your reading already contains the raw material. You just need AI to extract it and shape it into brand language.

üí° Authenticity is the brand The most powerful personal brands aren't manufactured ‚Äî they're excavated. Your reading reveals who you genuinely are, not who you think you should be. A brand built on your actual placements will feel effortless to maintain because you're not performing ‚Äî you're just being yourself, on purpose.

Create Brand Voice Guidelines

Your Mercury and Rising signs define your natural communication style. Use this follow-up prompt to turn that into actionable voice guidelines:

  • Mercury in Fire signs: Direct, passionate, exclamation-point energy. Your brand voice is bold and action-oriented.
  • Mercury in Earth signs: Practical, evidence-based, grounded. Your brand voice is trustworthy and no-nonsense.
  • Mercury in Air signs: Witty, idea-driven, conversational. Your brand voice is clever and socially aware.
  • Mercury in Water signs: Emotional, intuitive, storytelling. Your brand voice is empathetic and deep.

Ask AI: "Based on my Mercury sign and Rising sign, create a Brand Voice Guide that includes: tone words (3 words that describe how I sound), sentence style (short/long, formal/casual), what I'd NEVER say, and a sample paragraph written in my brand voice about any topic."

‚ö†Ô∏è Your voice should feel natural, not forced If the brand voice guidelines feel like you're putting on a costume, something's off. The goal is to amplify your natural style, not invent a new one. If AI suggests a tone that doesn't resonate, push back: "That feels too formal for me ‚Äî try again with more casual energy."

Build Your Brand One-Pager

Consolidate everything into a single reference document you can consult before writing any content, updating any bio, or making any brand decision:

  • Mission statement: One sentence from your brand DNA
  • Core values: Top 5, ranked
  • Brand archetype: Your compound archetype name
  • Voice guidelines: 3 tone words + what you never say
  • Origin story: 3-sentence arc
  • Tagline: Your favourite from the 5 options
  • Elevator pitch: 30-second version
  • Ready bios: One for each platform

Save this as a Google Doc, Notion page, or printed card. Every piece of content you create should pass the "brand one-pager test" ‚Äî if it doesn't align with your mission, values, and voice, it's off-brand. This document is your north star.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Copying someone else's brand energy. If your reading says you're a quiet, deep thinker (Water-dominant), don't try to brand yourself as a loud, disruptive firestarter because that's what gets likes. Inauthenticity is the fastest way to brand burnout.
  2. Skipping the origin story. People connect with vulnerability, not credentials. Your Chiron wound is your most powerful brand asset ‚Äî it's what makes you human and trustworthy.
  3. Making the brand about you instead of your audience. Your reading tells you WHO you are. Your brand should communicate what that means FOR the people you serve. Always connect inward insights to outward value.
  4. Using all the adjectives. Your brand voice should be 3 words, not 15. Clarity beats comprehensiveness. Pick 3 tone words and commit.
  5. Not updating annually. As you grow (especially along your North Node direction), your brand evolves too. Revisit your brand one-pager every 6-12 months to make sure it still fits.

How to Tell If AI Output Is Good

The 7-point checklist that separates useful AI responses from confident nonsense

üìñ 8 min read üìÖ February 2026 üéØ Beginner

AI always sounds confident. That's the problem. Whether it's giving you life-changing advice or making something up entirely, the tone is exactly the same. This guide teaches you how to tell the difference.

Why This Matters

AI doesn't know when it's wrong. It has no internal "confidence meter". A completely fabricated fact and a verified truth are delivered with the same polished certainty.

This means the quality filter has to be you. The good news: it's not hard once you know what to look for.

The 7-Point Quality Checklist

Run every important AI response through these 7 checks. It takes 60 seconds and saves you from bad decisions.

‚úÖ 1. Is it specific ‚Äî or vague?

Bad sign: The response is full of generalities that could apply to anyone.

Good sign: It includes specific details, numbers, examples, or names relevant to YOUR question.

‚ùå Vague (low quality)

"You should focus on improving your communication skills and being more organised."

Generic advice. Could be said to literally anyone.

‚úÖ Specific (high quality)

"With your Gemini Sun's natural verbal agility but Virgo Moon's tendency to over-analyse before speaking, try the '3-second rule' ‚Äî pause for 3 seconds before responding in heated conversations."

Tailored to the actual input. Gives an actionable technique.

‚úÖ 2. Can you verify the facts?

Bad sign: It mentions specific statistics, dates, or quotes that you can't find with a quick Google search.

Good sign: Factual claims check out, or the AI explicitly says "this is my interpretation" when being subjective.

üí° Quick verification trick: Copy any specific claim from an AI response, paste it into Google with quotes, and see if real sources confirm it. Takes 10 seconds.

‚úÖ 3. Is it well-structured?

Bad sign: One giant wall of text with no organisation.

Good sign: Clear headings, bullet points, numbered steps, or logical flow from intro ‚Üí details ‚Üí summary.

Note: If your output is a wall of text, that might be YOUR fault ‚Äî you probably didn't specify a format in your prompt. Learn the R-T-C-F formula ‚Üí

‚úÖ 4. Does it actually answer YOUR question?

Bad sign: The AI gives a thorough but generic answer about the topic, ignoring the specific angle you asked about.

Good sign: The response directly addresses your exact question, with your specific context woven throughout.

‚ö†Ô∏è Common trap: AI loves to give you "everything it knows about the topic" instead of just answering your question. If you asked about your Leo Sun's career strengths and got a general essay about Leo personality, the output isn't good enough ‚Äî ask it to be more specific.

‚úÖ 5. Does the tone match what you asked for?

Bad sign: You asked for a casual social media post and got a formal essay. Or you asked for professional copy and got emojis and slang.

Good sign: The tone, formality, and style match your request. If they don't ‚Äî iterate. Say "make it more casual" or "write this like a TikTok caption".

‚úÖ 6. Can you spot filler content?

Bad sign: Sentences that sound good but add zero information. AI has several tell-tale filler patterns:

  • "In today's fast-paced world..." (classic AI throat-clearing)
  • "It's important to remember that..." (stalling before the actual content)
  • "There are many ways to approach this..." (vague hedging)
  • Restating your question back to you in different words
  • Ending with "The possibilities are endless!" (empty enthusiasm)

Good sign: Every sentence adds new information or value. You couldn't delete a paragraph without losing something useful.

‚úÖ 7. The Friend Test

The ultimate quality check: Would you confidently share this with a friend as useful advice?

If you'd feel embarrassed sending it to someone ‚Äî if you'd add a caveat like "AI wrote this, so take it with a grain of salt" ‚Äî the output isn't good enough yet. Iterate until it is.

What to Do When Output Is Bad

Bad output doesn't mean the AI is broken. It usually means your prompt needs work. Try these fixes:

ProblemFix
Too vagueAdd specific context about your situation
Too genericSay "be specific ‚Äî give concrete examples"
Wrong toneSpecify the tone: "write casually, like texting a friend"
Too longSet a word limit: "respond in under 150 words"
Factually wrongTry Gemini (has web access) or say "double-check this"
Misunderstood the taskBreak your request into smaller, clearer steps
Full of fillerSay "remove all filler. Every sentence must add new info"

Applying This to Your Soul Blueprint Reading

When you use AI to explore your Soul Blueprint reading, use this checklist to evaluate the output:

  • Actionability: Does it give you something you can actually DO, not just think about?
  • Nuance: Does it acknowledge tensions or contradictions in your chart, or just paint a rosy picture?

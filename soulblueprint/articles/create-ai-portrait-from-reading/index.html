Craft Your Art Prompt

Now take the visual identity from Step 2 and ask AI to write a complete art prompt. This prompt can be used with any AI image tool, or simply as a creative brief for a Canva composition.

Using the visual identity you just created, write 3 different AI art prompts for a cosmic personality portrait. Each prompt should be:

- One detailed paragraph (60-100 words)
- Written in the style of a professional AI image prompt
- Include: subject description, setting, lighting, color palette, mood, art style, and composition

Create the Portrait

You have several free options for bringing your portrait to life:

Option A: Canva Composition (Easiest)

Use your AI-generated visual identity as a guide to build a symbolic portrait in Canva:

  1. Open Canva and create a square design (1080√ó1080px)
  2. Set a background that matches the setting (search Canva's photo library for the keywords AI suggested)
  3. Layer symbolic elements: search for the objects AI listed (moon, compass, flames, etc.) and arrange them as a collage
  4. Add color overlays matching the hex codes from your palette
  5. Add the person's archetype title in an elegant font
  6. Apply Canva filters for a cohesive, atmospheric look

Style and Refine Your Portrait

Whether you used Canva or an AI image generator, spend a few minutes refining:

  • Color correction: In Canva, use the Adjust panel to shift hues toward your personality palette. Warm for fire signs, cool for water, earthy for earth, light and airy for air.
  • Add text overlay: Place the archetype name (e.g., "The Celestial Navigator") in an elegant font at the bottom. Use a semi-transparent background strip for readability.
  • Add a border: A simple 1-2px border or a gradient frame ties the composition together.
  • Try multiple crops: Generate a square version (Instagram), a vertical version (Pinterest/phone wallpaper), and a wide version (desktop wallpaper).

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Being too vague with the prompt. "A portrait of a creative person" won't produce anything interesting. Use the specific symbols, colors, and settings from your reading. Specificity = striking results.
  2. Going for photorealism. Symbolic and artistic portraits are more meaningful (and more shareable) than attempts at realistic photos. Lean into the fantasy.
  3. Ignoring contrast. A portrait that's all one color or mood feels flat. Make sure there's visual tension ‚Äî light against dark, warm accent in a cool scene, sharp detail against soft blur.
  4. Forgetting the personality connection. Every visual element should trace back to something in the reading. If someone asks "why the crescent moon?", you should be able to say "because my Moon Sign reading described..."
  5. Not iterating. Your first result rarely matches what you envisioned. Generate 3-4 variations and combine the best elements. Professional artists always iterate.

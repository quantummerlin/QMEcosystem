Build Your Slides

Open your chosen tool and follow these steps:

In Google Slides:

  1. Go to slides.google.com ‚Üí Blank Presentation
  2. Set the slide size to Widescreen (16:9) under File ‚Üí Page setup
  3. Apply a theme from the Themes panel, or use a simple dark background
  4. Create one slide per section from your AI outline
  5. Copy the title, text, and bullet points from the AI output into each slide

In Canva:

  1. Search "presentation" in Canva ‚Üí pick a template you like
  2. Delete slides you don't need, duplicate ones you do
  3. Replace placeholder text with your AI-generated content
  4. Adjust colors to match your reading's vibe (or use the Soul Blueprint palette: #667eea ‚Üí #764ba2)
‚ö†Ô∏è Less text = better slides Each slide should have a max of 25 words visible. If you find yourself writing paragraphs, move the extra text to speaker notes. Audiences remember images and short phrases, not blocks of text.

Design Tips for a Polished Look

You don't need to be a designer. Follow these simple rules and your slideshow will look professional:

  • One idea per slide. Don't combine strengths and challenges on the same slide.
  • Use large fonts. Title: 36pt+. Body text: 24pt+. If you can't read it from 3 feet away, it's too small.
  • Consistent color palette. Pick 2-3 colors and use them everywhere. The Soul Blueprint gradient (#667eea ‚Üí #764ba2) works beautifully as a background or accent.
  • Add imagery. Use Canva's free photo library or search Unsplash for cosmic, nature, or abstract images that match each slide's theme.
  • Use icons. Both Canva and Google Slides support icons ‚Äî use them next to bullet points for quick visual scanning.

You can also ask AI to recommend specific visual elements. Try this quick follow-up prompt:

üìã Visual recommendations prompt 
Based on the slide content you created, suggest for each slide:

1. A background image keyword I can search on Unsplash or Canva
2. An emoji or icon that represents the slide's theme
3. A color accent (hex code) that matches the mood

Keep suggestions cosmic, modern, and visually warm. Use a palette anchored around #667eea and #764ba2.

Export, Present, and Share

Once your slides look great, here's how to get them out into the world:

  • Present live: In Google Slides, hit the Present button. In Canva, click Present ‚Üí Standard.
  • Share as a link: Both tools let you generate a shareable link. Great for texting someone their reading.
  • Download as PDF: Perfect for a keepsake or print gift. In Canva: Share ‚Üí Download ‚Üí PDF Standard.
  • Download as video: Canva lets you export presentations as MP4 videos with auto-play timing ‚Äî ideal for Instagram Reels or TikTok.
  • Embed on a website: Google Slides can be embedded with an iframe. Great if you have a blog or portfolio.
üí° Gift idea Turn the slideshow into a PDF and print it as a small booklet at a local print shop. It makes a surprisingly personal birthday or holiday gift.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Too much text per slide. If a slide has more than 25 words of visible text, move the rest to speaker notes. Slideshows are visual ‚Äî not documents.
  2. No narrative arc. Your slides should tell a story: who they are ‚Üí their gifts ‚Üí their challenges ‚Üí their path forward. Don't just dump facts in random order.
  3. Inconsistent design. Pick one template and stick with it. Don't mix fonts, styles, or color palettes across slides.
  4. Skipping the title slide. The title slide sets the tone. Include the person's name, a short tagline from the reading, and an atmospheric image.
  5. Forgetting speaker notes. Even if you're sharing the PDF, speaker notes add context. They're especially helpful if someone else will present the deck.

There are dozens of ways to understand your personality. Some have decades of research behind them. Others are brand new. This guide compares the major approaches honestly ‚Äî so you can pick the right tool for what you actually need.

The Big 4 Personality Frameworks

1. MBTI (Myers-Briggs Type Indicator)

What it is: Sorts you into one of 16 types based on 4 dimensions: Introversion/Extraversion, Sensing/Intuition, Thinking/Feeling, Judging/Perceiving.

Strengths: Easy to understand, widely known, great for team dynamics and career discussions.

Limitations: Binary categories (you're either Thinking OR Feeling, with no middle ground). Low test-retest reliability ‚Äî many people get different results on retakes. Not strongly supported by academic psychology research.

2. Big Five (OCEAN)

What it is: Measures you on 5 spectrums: Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism. Each is a scale, not a binary.

Strengths: The most scientifically validated personality model. Used in actual psychological research. Nuanced (you get a score on each dimension, not a label).

Limitations: Less "fun" and memorable than MBTI. Harder to use in casual conversation. Doesn't address deeper questions about purpose or growth.

3. Enneagram

What it is: Identifies your core motivation through one of 9 types, each with healthy, average, and unhealthy expressions. Includes "wings" and growth/stress patterns.

Strengths: Excellent for understanding motivations (not just behaviours). The growth paths are genuinely useful. Popular in therapy and coaching.

Limitations: Limited scientific validation. Self-typing can be unreliable. Some people don't fit neatly into one type.

4. AI + Soul Blueprint

What it is: Generates a multi-layered personality profile from birth data (astrology + numerology), then uses AI tools to explore, analyse, and apply the results interactively.

Strengths: Extremely detailed (20+ data points). Infinitely explorable with AI. Covers personality, relationships, career, growth, and timing. Free.

Limitations: Based on astrological/numerological frameworks, not empirical psychology. Should be used as a self-reflection tool, not a diagnostic one.

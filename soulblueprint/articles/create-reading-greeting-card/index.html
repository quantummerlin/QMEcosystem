Design the Card in Canva

Open Canva and search for "greeting card" or "birthday card" templates. Pick a design that matches the person's vibe:

  • Minimalist typography cards ‚Äî perfect for Earth and Air signs who appreciate clean design
  • Celestial/starry designs ‚Äî great for Water signs and anyone who loves the cosmic aesthetic
  • Bold, colourful cards ‚Äî ideal for Fire signs who want energy and warmth
  • Floral or nature cards ‚Äî beautiful for Earth-dominant readings

Canva card templates are typically 5 √ó 7 inches (fold card format) with a front and inside page. Replace the template text with your AI-generated content. Keep the front dramatic and the inside personal.

Design tips: Use no more than 2 fonts. Match the colour palette to their element ‚Äî warm golds for Fire, forest greens for Earth, sky blues for Air, deep teals for Water. Add their zodiac symbol as a small decorative element.

Print and Fold at Home

Download your card from Canva as PDF Print for best quality. Then print:

  • Paper: Use heavy card stock (200‚Äì300 gsm). Standard copy paper is too flimsy for a card. Card stock is available at any office supply store or Amazon for about $8‚Äì12 per pack.
  • Printing: Print double-sided if your printer supports it. If not, print the front and inside separately, then glue back-to-back before folding.
  • Folding: Score the fold line first using a ruler and a bone folder (or the back of a butter knife). This creates a crisp, clean fold instead of a cracked crease.
  • Finishing: Trim any white edges with a paper cutter or sharp scissors. Slide the card into an envelope ‚Äî A7 envelopes (5.25 √ó 7.25 in) fit standard 5 √ó 7 cards.
‚ö†Ô∏è Test print on regular paper first Before using your nice card stock, do a test print on regular paper. Check that the front and inside align properly when folded, text isn't cut off, and colours look right. Card stock is too expensive to waste on test prints.

Digital Card Options

If you're sending the card digitally (or the person is far away), you have several options:

  • Image card: Download from Canva as a high-res PNG and text or email it directly. Simple and immediate.
  • Animated card: Use Canva's animation features to add subtle movement ‚Äî floating stars, gentle gradient shifts, or text reveal effects. Download as an MP4 and send via messaging apps.
  • Interactive card: Use a free platform like Canva Websites to create a one-page web card with a shareable link. The recipient clicks the link and sees a beautiful full-screen card.
  • E-card services: Upload your Canva design to Paperless Post or Greenvelope for a polished digital delivery experience with RSVP tracking.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Writing too much inside the card. A card message should be 50‚Äì80 words, not 200. If you have more to say, write a separate letter and tuck it inside the card.
  2. Using generic astrology language. "You're such a Scorpio" is lazy. "Your Scorpio intensity is exactly why people trust you with their deepest secrets" is personal. Always ground the message in specific reading insights.
  3. Printing on thin paper. Anything under 200 gsm will feel flimsy and cheap. Card stock is non-negotiable for a physical card.
  4. Forgetting the envelope. A beautiful card deserves a proper envelope. A7 envelopes are standard and cost pennies. Don't hand someone a bare card.
  5. Overdesigning the card. The message is the star ‚Äî the design should frame it, not compete with it. Keep visuals simple and let the words do the emotional heavy lifting.

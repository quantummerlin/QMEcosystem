Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Shorts over 50 seconds. Watch-through rate is the #1 factor in YouTube's Shorts algorithm. A 30-second Short that people watch twice beats a 60-second Short that people abandon at 40 seconds. Keep it tight.
  2. No text overlays or captions. Most Shorts are watched without sound initially. If your video is voice-only with no text on screen, the majority of viewers scroll past. Add key phrases as text overlays on every Short.
  3. Generic titles. "Zodiac Video #4" tells YouTube nothing about your content. "What Your Scorpio Moon Says About Your Love Life" tells the algorithm exactly who to show it to. Be specific and keyword-rich.
  4. Not using the end screen. The last 2 seconds of your Short should include a subscribe prompt ‚Äî either verbal ("Hit subscribe for more zodiac truth") or visual (pointing to a subscribe button). This is your conversion moment.
  5. Posting once and quitting. YouTube Shorts growth is exponential, not linear. Your first 20 Shorts might get 100 views each. Then one hits 50K. Then the algorithm starts pushing everything. Consistency through the slow period is what separates growing channels from abandoned ones.

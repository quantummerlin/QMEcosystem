If you're reading this, you probably just ran your first reading. Maybe a life path number. Maybe a birth chart. Maybe you landed on the compatibility tool because someone told you about it and you were curious.

And you're feeling one of two things.

Either *"holy shit, how did it know that?"* — or *"this is interesting but I'm not sure what to do with it."*

Both are exactly right. And honestly? Most people swing between those two feelings for a while. I still do, and I've been building these tools for years.

Let me walk you through what I'd do if I were starting fresh. Not the "recommended user flow" — I'm not going to market to you. This is just what I wish someone had told me when I first started looking at my own numbers.

---

## Start with Your Life Path Number

Go to the [main page](/) and enter your birth date. The first thing you'll see is your **Life Path Number**. It's the most foundational piece of your cosmic profile — think of it as the bassline of a song. Everything else plays over the top of it, but this is the rhythm underneath.

When you read yours, don't ask "is this me?" Ask a better question: *"when has this been me?"*

Because your Life Path isn't a personality type. It's a pattern — one that shows up in the decisions you keep making, the relationships you keep choosing, and the struggles that keep finding you. It's not about who you are on a good day. It's about the thread running through all your days.

I'm a Life Path 7. The Seeker. When I first read mine, I thought it was generic. Then I looked at the last decade of my life — the obsessive research, the need to understand things at a level that annoyed everyone around me, the way I'd disappear into projects for months. It wasn't generic at all. It was uncomfortably specific.

## Then Check Your Full Birth Chart

Once you've read your Life Path, you'll see a button to generate your full reading. This is where it gets interesting.

Your full chart combines:

- **Western Astrology** — Sun sign, Moon sign, Rising sign, and all the planets
- **Numerology** — Life Path, Destiny Number, Soul Urge, Expression Number
- **Chinese Zodiac** — Your animal, your element, your yin/yang
- **Moon Phase** — The exact phase of the moon the night you were born

Each of these systems was developed independently, by different civilisations, across different centuries. They shouldn't agree with each other. But when you run them all on the same birth date — your birth date — they tell the same story from different angles.

That's the thing that got me. Not any one system on its own. The *convergence*. When your numerology says "you need freedom" and your astrology says "you have Sagittarius Moon" and your Chinese zodiac says "you're a Horse element" — and none of those systems were designed to talk to each other — something is going on that I can't fully explain. And I've stopped trying to explain it. I just build the tools and let people see for themselves.

## The Compatibility Tool

Here's where most people get hooked, honestly.

Run your birth date alongside someone you know well — a partner, a best friend, a sibling, a parent. Someone where the relationship is complex.

The [compatibility tool](/compatibility.html) doesn't just say "you're 78% compatible." It breaks down **seven dimensions** of your connection — emotional resonance, intellectual compatibility, life path alignment, Chinese zodiac harmony, and more. Each one scored independently.

What I find fascinating is the gaps. When two people score 95% on emotional resonance but 40% on communication style — that's not a bug. That's the exact dynamic you've been living with. You feel deeply connected to this person but you keep having the same miscommunication. The numbers just showed you why.

I built this tool after trying to understand a friendship that made no sense on paper. We had nothing in common. Different signs, different life paths, different backgrounds. But we'd been inseparable for twelve years. When I ran both our charts through every system, I found the connection buried in a place I'd never thought to look — our Soul Urge numbers were identical. The part of us that nobody else sees matched perfectly. The rest was just noise.

## What to Watch For

Here's the thing nobody tells you about this stuff.

The first reading is for recognition. *"Oh, that's me."* That's the hook, and it's real, but it's not the valuable part.

The valuable part comes when you return. Not tomorrow — give it a few weeks. Maybe a month. Come back and read the same chart after something has happened in your life. A decision. A conflict. A win. A loss. Something that made you question yourself.

Read it again with that experience in mind.

I designed these readings to work like that — like a good book you pick up every few years and see something different in. The words haven't changed. You have. And each time, a different section will land differently.

> My mother used to say that about her tarot cards. "The cards don't change. You do. That's why the same spread gives you different answers." I didn't understand that when I was twelve. I understand it now.

## The Daily Forecast

If you want to make this a habit — and this is genuinely optional, no pressure — the [daily forecast](/forecasts.html) is where most returning visitors end up.

It's not a horoscope. It's calculated from your actual birth data — your personal day number, your current planetary transits, your biorhythm cycle. It changes literally every day because the maths changes every day.

Some people check it in the morning like they check the weather. I do, and I built the thing. Not because I think the stars control my day, but because it's a useful lens. If my forecast says "communication challenges today" and I've got a difficult meeting — I'm going in a little more prepared. Not because the cosmos told me to. Because the pattern made me pay attention.
